# Hospitality & Travel Trends: 2026 Signal Scan

## Travel & Lodging Trends

**The "Whycation":** In 2026, travel will start with a specific purpose (personal passions, skills, or hobbies) rather than just a destination.

**Hushpitality:** A growing trend where travelers seek "sweet silence" and quiet moments, even during group trips.

**Decision Detox:** Travelers are opting for "decision-free" getaways to combat cognitive overload, favoring all-inclusive, curated experiences where experts handle all logistics.

**Pet-Centric Travel:** The "Pawprint Economy" is rising, with 64% of pet owners prioritizing their pet's needs when booking. New "human-grade" travel experiences for pets include long-haul transpacific in-cabin flights.

## Transient Shelter Economics

**Hotels as Strategic Assets:** Meetings and events (MICE) now represent over 25% of global revenue for major hotel groups, providing stability against fluctuating B2C demand.

**Luxury & Economy Resilience:** Investors are showing the greatest interest in luxury and economy hotel tiers.

**Midwest Appeal:** 63% of travelers are increasingly interested in the U.S. Midwest for its "low-key cool" and affordability.

## Wellness, Rest & Sleep

**Radical Rest:** A shift toward "sauna-vations" and finding "light in the dark," focusing on restorative experiences like floating saunas and soundbaths.

**The Sleep Priority:** 90% of road-trippers cite a comfortable bed as the most important hotel amenity.

**Wellness as Necessity:** Wellness is being reframed from a luxury to a functional tool for self-regulation and "emotional resets".

**Gut Health Focus:** A move toward in-home fermentation and advanced nutritional tracking to manage mental clarity and immune strength.

## Food & Gathering

**Table Captains:** 72% of diners prefer visiting new restaurants with a "Table Captain" — a friend who takes charge of ordering and dietary needs to improve the social experience.

**Communal Dining:** 90% of Gen Z diners enjoy communal tables, viewing them as a way to meet new people and have "meet-cute" moments.

**The "Me-Me Economy":** A rise in intentional solo dining, where nearly half of eating moments are now solo, driven by a desire for self-care and personalization.

**TV Dinners 2.0:** A revival of eating while watching TV as a "cultural campfire" moment, but with healthier, adaptogen-filled, and "story-led" meal options.

**Functional Rituals:** Everyday consumption is turning into "personal ceremony," with the functional food and beverage market projected to reach $793.6B by 2032.

## Experience & Belonging

**Fueled by Fandom:** 80% of consumers find joy in fandoms. Brands are creating immersive experiences, like themed pop-ups (e.g., Barbie Café), to build emotional bonds and "move with" these communities.

**Skills Over Souvenirs:** 31% of Gen Z travelers prefer returning from a trip with a new skill rather than a physical souvenir; workshop bookings have increased by 59%.

**Shared Memories:** Live experiences and "daytime raves" are fulfilling a profound human need for connection and healthier social alternatives to alcohol-centered nightlife.

**Curated Collectivism:** A shift toward intimate, expert-led gatherings built around shared passions rather than massive, anonymous spectacles.
